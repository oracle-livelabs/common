# Richard Lanham Scoring Cheatsheet

Apply these criteria to each workshop markdown file.

## Process
1. Read the file to understand purpose and audience.
2. Highlight sentences longer than 20 words or with two or more prepositional phrases.
3. Flag passive constructions unless subject emphasis clearly requires them.
4. Identify nominalizations and consider verb-based rewrites.
5. Detect contractions and replace them with full forms.
6. Check Oxford commas and Chicago-style punctuation.
7. Avoid em dashes; prefer commas, colons, or periods.
8. Confirm each paragraph carries one idea and ends with a concrete action or result.

## Scoring Rubric (0-5)
- 5 - Exemplary: active voice, vivid verbs, concise sentences, no meaningful rule breaches.
- 4 - Strong: minor lapses such as one passive or long sentence, but overall tight prose.
- 3 - Adequate: several issues such as passive voice or wordiness, but the meaning stays clear.
- 2 - Needs Work: frequent official-style habits, unclear actors, or inconsistent mechanics.
- 1 - Poor: dense jargon, many passive verbs, missing Oxford commas, or inconsistent tone.
- 0 - Critical: structural breakdown or pervasive rule violations that block comprehension.

Record infractions with line numbers or direct quotes so the author can revise quickly.
