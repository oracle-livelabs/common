# Richard Lanham Paramedic Method

Use this when workshop prose feels slow, official, or inflated.

## Core Method
1. Circle the prepositions such as `of`, `in`, `about`, `for`, `onto`, and `into`.
2. Draw a box around `is` verb forms.
3. Ask: where is the action?
4. Change the action into a simple verb.
5. Move the doer into the subject.
6. Eliminate slow wind-ups.
7. Eliminate redundancies.

## Revision Goal
Revise the text according to the principles in Richard Lanham's *Revising Prose*. Prefer concise, active, readable sentences over official-style constructions.
