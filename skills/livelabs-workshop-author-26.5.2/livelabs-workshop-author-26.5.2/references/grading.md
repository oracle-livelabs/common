# General Grading Guide

## Process
- Act as an expert writer and editor.
- Grade the document or passage using an ABCDF scale.
- You may append `+` or `-` to any grade except `F`.
- Grade one layer at a time.
- Begin with layer `n=1`.
- Do not proceed to layer `n+1` unless layer `n` scores an `A`.
- For workshop-authoring self-review, map the result to an internal 1-5 quality bar and revise until the changed files are at least 3/5, with a target of 4/5 or 5/5.

## Output Format
- Format the output using Markdown.
- Each layer gets its own `##` heading.
- If the layer grade is `A` or better, provide a one-line affirmation.
- If the grade is below `A`, provide specific feedback:
  - list all faulty sentences
  - show each faulty sentence verbatim
  - suggest exactly how to improve it

## Ground Rules
- Follow Chicago Manual of Style unless instructed otherwise.
- If constraints conflict, report the conflict explicitly.
- If asked to grade a `.docx` file, evaluate it as if all proposed edits have been accepted.

## Spelling, Punctuation, and Grammar
- Spell words according to AHD.
- Adhere to CMOS.
- Use Oxford commas.
- Use nonbreaking spaces where CMOS requires them.
- Use proper typesetting for hyphens, non-breaking hyphens, en dashes, and em dashes.
- Use grammatically parallel form and structure in lists.
- Use title case only for proper nouns, such as brand names.

## Clarity, Engagement, and Voice
- Use clear and direct verb-style writing.
- Use bold, confident statements.
- Use active voice; avoid passive voice.
- Favor sentences with reduced word counts.
- Use Lanham's Paramedic Method to cut official-style writing.
- Do not use weasel words.
- Use short words when they work.
- Avoid uncommon words unless they are necessary.
- Do not disrupt rhythm.
- Keep sentences short.
- Use short, enticing titles.
- Use storytelling or examples when they help.
- Avoid the weak sentence pattern `__ weak-verb __, gerund __`.

## Structure and Flow
- Make each passage the passage the reader expected next.
- Do not transition abruptly.
- Keep paragraphs short enough to feel readable.
- Use lists to clarify structure.
- Do not nest lists.

## Relevance
- Meet the reader where the reader lives.
- Make the reader feel heard and understood.
- Keep the content relevant to the reader's actual interests.

## Correctness and Defensibility
- Never lie.
- Make statements true, correct, and defensible.
- Do not make untestable claims.
- Do not create conjectures and leave them unresolved.
