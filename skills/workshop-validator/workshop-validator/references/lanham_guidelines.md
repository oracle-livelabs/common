# Richard Lanham Scoring Cheatsheet

Apply these criteria to each markdown file.

## Process
1. Read the file to understand purpose and audience.
2. Highlight sentences exceeding 20 words or containing two or more prepositional phrases.
3. Flag passive constructions (`be` + past participle) unless subject emphasis demands it.
4. Identify nominalizations (nouns built from verbs) and consider verb-based rewrites.
5. Detect contractions; replace with full forms.
6. Ensure Oxford commas and Chicago punctuation.
7. Avoid em dashes; use commas, colons, or periods.
8. Confirm each paragraph delivers one idea and ends with a concrete action or result.

## Scoring Rubric (0–5)
- **5 – Exemplary**: Active voice, vivid verbs, concise sentences, no rule breaches.
- **4 – Strong**: Minor lapses (one passive or long sentence) but overall tight prose.
- **3 – Adequate**: Several issues (passive voice, wordiness) yet meaning stays clear.
- **2 – Needs Work**: Frequent official-style habits, unclear actors, or inconsistent mechanics.
- **1 – Poor**: Dense jargon, many passive verbs, missing Oxford commas, inconsistent tone.
- **0 – Critical**: Structural breakdown or pervasive rule violations that block comprehension.

Record infractions with line numbers or direct quotes so authors can revise quickly.
